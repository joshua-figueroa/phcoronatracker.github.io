Pandemic Timeline

Pandemic Timeline

Dec 31

Dec 31

The World Health Organization (WHO) reported clustering of pneumonia cases in Wuhan City, Hubei Province, China.

Jan 6

Jan 6

DOH ordered strict screening of incoming travelers.

Jan 7

Jan 7

China identified the new type of coronavirus, the 2019 Novel Coronavirus Acute Respiratory Disease (2019-nCoV ARD).

Jan 11

Jan 11

DOH urged the public with a history of travel to China to seek immediate medical consultation.

Jan 17

Jan 17

DOH implemented the mandatory accomplishment of Health Declaration Cards for all port arrivals.

DOH coordinated with DFA to inform the Chinese Embassy on the number of persons under investigation(PUIs).

Jan 20

Jan 20

DOH established the nCoV Task Force.

Jan 21

Jan 21

DOH related Health Advisories and issued the Interm Guidelines on the Preparedness and Response to the 2019 Novel Coronavirus.

Jan 23

Jan 23

Airports and seaports were on tight watch against the threat of 2019-nCoV ARD.

The Civil Aeronautics Board indefinitely suspended all operations from Wuhan to the Philippines.

Jan 28

Jan 28

DOH convened the first Inter-Agency Task Force for the Management of Emerging Infectious Diseases(IATF-EID) Meeting.

Repatriation of Filipinos was processed.

The Bureau of Immigration ordered the suspension of the issuance of visas upon arrival to passport holders from China.

Jan 29

Jan 29

DOH convened all Centers for Health Development Directors and concerned chiefs of hospitals to attend all persons under investigation and monitoring (PUI/PUM)for 2019-nCoV ARD.

Jan 30

Jan 30

DOH Confirmed the first 2019-nCoV ARD case in the Philippines, and initiated contact-tracing of the confirmed patient.

DFA issued a public advisory calling for the voluntary repatriation of Filipinos in Hubei.

LTFRB mandated all drivers and conductors of public utility vehicles to wear face masks.

Jan 31

Jan 31

The President issued a travel ban to foreigners coming from Hubei and anywhere in China where there is a spread of the disease.

WHO declared the 2019-nCoV ARD as a "Public Health Emergency of International Concern".

DILG released a Memorandum Circular No. 2020-018 indicating guides to action against Coronavirus.

Research Institute for Tropical Medicine (RITM) established capability in confirming 2019-nCoV ARD infection.

DOLE issued guidelines on 2019-nCoV ARD prevention and control at the workplace.

DTI ordered the Philippine International Trading Corporation to start sourcing 5 million masks to augment the local supply, especially the needs of DOH and other health workers.

Feb 1

Feb 1

Education officials created a task for the Management of the Department of Education Response to 2019-nCoV ARD.

Feb 2

Feb 2

DOH confirmed the first death of 2019-nCoV ARD case in the Philippines.

DOH identified the second case of 2019-nCoV ARD.

Feb 3

Feb 3

DOH held a consultative meeting with professional medical societies on the 2019-nCoV ARD Decision Tool and Other Essential Guidelines.

PRRD presided over a situation briefing on the 2019-nCoV ARD in Malaca√±ang.

DOH identified the second case of 2019-nCoV ARD.

Feb 5

Feb 5

DOH confirmed the third 2019-nCoV ARD case.

A house committee hearing on the 2019-nCoV was held.

Feb 9

Feb 9

DFA reported 32 Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) from Wuhan, China returned to the Philippines.

Thirty(30) Filipino repatriates from Wuhan, China, arrived in the Philippines to undergo mandatory 14-day quarantine at the Athletes' Village in New Clark City, Capas, Tarlac.

Feb 11

Feb 11

WHO renamed 2019-nCoV ARD as Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19).

Philippine Health Insurance (PhilHealth) issued a circular regarding the enhancement of packages related to COVID-19.

Feb 14

Feb 14

IATF-EID lifted the travel restrictions imposed on Taiwan.

PRRD encouraged everyone through a video message to travel with him around the country assuring the people that it is safe to travel as systems and protocols are in place to safeguard the well-being of our travelers.

Feb 18

Feb 18

IATF-EID lifted the departure travel ban for Overseas Filipino Workers, student visa holders, and permanent residents of HongKong and Macau.

PRRD encouraged everyone through a video message to travel with him around the country assuring the people that it is safe to travel as systems and protocols are in place to safeguard the well-being of our travelers.

Feb 19

Feb 19

DepEd issued Memorandum 23 to resume the conduct of national, regional, and/or off-campus activities starting February 24.

Feb 23

Feb 23

DFA confirmed one Filipino tested positive for COVID-19 in Singapore.

Feb 25

Feb 25

DFA welcomed a total of 445 seafarers from MV Diamond Princess.

Feb 26

Feb 26

IATF-EID imposed temporary travel restrictions to South Korea.

Mar 3

Mar 3

The Bureau of Quarantine issued guidelines at all seaports for prevention and spreadof COVID-19.

Mar 4

Mar 4

IATF-EID decided to partially lift the travel restrictions to South Korea.

DOLE released Guidelines on the implementation of Flexible Work Arrangements as Remidial Measure Due to the Ongoing Outbreak of COVID-19.

Mar 6

Mar 6

DOH confirmed two more cases of COVID-19 in the country, bringing the total of positive cases to 5.

DOH released WHO interim guidelines for health workers' exposure risk assessment and management in the context of COVID-19.

Mar 7

Mar 7

DOH reported the first case of local transmission for the country's sixth positive case.

DOH released Public Advisory No. 13 regarding updated guidelines for management and referral of suspected and confirmed COVID-19 cases under Code red.

DOH released Public Advisory No. 14 regarding clarifications on management and referral of close contacts of confirmed patients.

Mar 9

Mar 9

IATF-EID released Resolution No. 10 on Recommendations for the Management of the COVID-19 Situation.

The Department of Tourism released a statement in view of the virus outbreak.

The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) allowed use of COVID-19 test kits developed by local scientists.

Mar 10

Mar 10

The National Privacy Commission reminded DOH to be very careful in releasing COVID-19 patient information to the public.

Mar 12

Mar 12

PRRD ordered strict community quarantine in the NCR from March 15 until April 12, suspending all classes and government work in NCR.

FDA declared prohibition on the online selling of essential emergency medical supplies beyond the price ceiling.

FDA set prohibition of selling COVID-19 testing kit without authorization.

Mar 13

Mar 13

DepEd released guidelines in computing the final grades of the students in Metro Manila and other areas affected by the virus outbreak.

DepEd issued a memorandum regarding adoption of video conferencing systems during the community quarantine period due to COVID-19 to continue operations and communications.

Mar 16

Mar 16

PRRD declared a State of Calamity in the country for the next six months due to COVID-19 through Proclamation No. 929.

PRRD announced an enhanced community quarantine (ECQ) in the entire luzon.

Government economic team rolled out the PHP27.1 billion package against the pandemic.

Mar 17

Mar 17

DOH reported the first recovered COVID-19 case in the country.

Palace issued a Memorandum from the Executive Secretary on Community Quarantine over the entire Luzon and further guidelines for the management of the COVID-19.
